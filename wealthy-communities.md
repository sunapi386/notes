# Notable Wealthy Communities

A comparison of ultra-wealthy enclaves in California, the USA, and Canada.

## Montecito vs Atherton

Two of the richest communities in the US with very different characters.

**Montecito, CA** (Santa Barbara County) — coastal, Mediterranean feel. Entertainment/media wealth. Oprah, Harry & Meghan, Ellen DeGeneres, Katy Perry, Gwyneth Paltrow. Median home price $3M–$6M+, many estates $10M–$50M+. Population ~9,000.

**Atherton, CA** (San Mateo County) — consistently ranked #1 most expensive zip code in the US. Tech/VC wealth. No commercial districts, no sidewalks, purely residential. Median home $6M–$8M+, many $20M+. Population ~7,000.

| | Montecito | Atherton |
|---|---|---|
| Wealth source | Entertainment, media, old money | Tech, venture capital, finance |
| Feel | Coastal, Mediterranean, resort-like | Suburban, wooded, deliberately plain |
| Celebrity factor | Very high (Hollywood/media) | Low (tech moguls prefer anonymity) |
| Scenery | Ocean + mountains, stunning | Oak trees + manicured lots, pleasant but flat |
| Proximity to | Santa Barbara / LA | San Francisco / Palo Alto / Stanford |
| Climate | Mild coastal, warmer | Mild but foggier/cooler |

## Common Patterns

Ultra-wealthy enclaves tend to share: no commercial zoning, large lots, mature trees for privacy, proximity (but not too close) to a major city, and often natural beauty as a draw. Newer ones (Jackson Hole, Indian Creek, Paradise Valley) are growing due to state tax advantages and remote work flexibility.
